The Japanese Kimono

Many nations have traditional styles of dress. These are the country's traditional fashions. When you think of Japan, one or two images may come to mind. Maybe you think of delicious sushi? Or mountains and temples? Or maybe you think about the traditional Japanese dress, the Kimono?

Origin

Kimonos came to Japan from China. They were worn underneath clothes as an undergarment. "Kimono" was the Japanese word for clothing. Now however, the word "kimono" refers to one type of Japanese dress. Kimonos are robes with long sleeves. The sleeves are very wide. Kimonos are also shaped like a "T." People wear a very wide belt with their kimonos. This belt is called an "obi." They are traditionally wrapped around your body from left to right. In fact, only the dead are wrapped right to left!

Style

As you will learn, kimonos are a very important part of Japanese history. Kimonos became popular during the Heian period. Before that, Japanese people wore a different style of clothing. They wore pants and shirts of different materials. During the Heian period, a different type of clothing began being made. People began cutting clothes in straight lines and sewing them together. This style of making clothes gave makers plenty of freedom. Seamstresses didn't have to worry about size or body shape. Kimonos are made in all the same size for women, and all the same size for men. They are folded and tucked to make them fit each person. Thick fabrics could be used for winter and thin linen for summer.

Kimonos became very popular for a long time. As they began getting more popular, people became interested in different colors and fabrics. They matched bright fabrics with seasons. Also, in the past you could match colors to social and political class. By looking at someone's kimono, you could tell what part of society they belonged to.

In the western world and other countries, people wear a lot of accessories. They may wear jewelry like rings and necklaces. The Japanese did not dress like this, however. Instead, they expressed their style and taste with the fabric of their kimono. Kimono designers used their fabric as a canvas. Like an artist who paints pictures, people would design very elegant images on their fabric. Some kimonos were even painted with ink!

Warriors on the battlefield also wore very bright and colorful kimonos. Soldiers paid lots of attention to their dress. They wore extravagant outfits! The color of a warrior's kimono showed what part of the army he belonged to. At one point in time, kimonos involved 25 different layers of fabric! Can you imagine having to put on 25 layers of clothes? While soldiers and government officials dressed in particular colors to show class, normal citizens wore kimonos to show their family. They would put their family crest on their kimonos. Married women had a different style of kimono than single women. Mothers also had to teach their daughters how to wear kimonos. Nowadays, there are schools that teach you how to properly wear a kimono.

Kimono Ceremony

Kimonos are still worn for traditional ceremonies, as well. You may find Japanese couples getting married wearing kimonos. They are also worn to funerals. The Japanese wear black kimonos to funerals. And men getting married wear black kimonos too. In the past, women wore kimonos while doing traditional arts such as tea ceremonies. They are often worn now to summer festivals. The western world has adopted the traditional Japanese kimono. If you go into a clothing store in the United States and ask for a kimono, you will be given a long light fabric robe.

Now the most popular fabric to make kimonos with is silk. Silk is an expensive fabric. The cost of the fabric has made kimonos much less popular these days. A kimono could cost you thousands of dollars! Today if you travel to Japan, you will not see as many as you did in the past. However, traditional kimono fabric designers are considered very important in Japan. They create unique masterpieces.

Kelib chiqishi (Origin)

Kimonolar Yaponiyaga Xitoydan kirib kelgan. Ular kiyimlar tagidan ichki kiyim sifatida kiyilgan. "Kimono" yaponcha kiyim degan so'z edi. Hozir esa, "Kimono" so'zi yapon libosining bir turini anglatadi. Kimonolar uzun yengli xalatlardir. Yenglari juda keng. Kimonolar shuningdek "T" shaklida bo'ladi. Odamlar kimonolari bilan juda keng kamar taqishadi. Bu kamar "obi" deb ataladi. Ular an'anaviy ravishda tanangizga chapdan o'ngga qarab o'raladi. Aslida, faqat o'liklar o'ngdan chapga o'raladi!

Uslub (Style)

Bilib olganingizdek, kimonolar Yaponiya tarixining juda muhim qismidir. Kimonolar Xeyan davrida ommalashgan. Undan oldin, yapon xalqi boshqacha uslubdagi kiyimlarni kiyishgan. Ular turli materiallardan shim va ko'ylaklar kiyishgan. Xeyan davrida kiyim tikishning boshqacha turi paydo bo'ldi. Odamlar kiyimlarni to'g'ri chiziqlar bo'ylab kesishni va ularni birga tikishni boshladilar. Kiyim tikishning bu uslubi tikuvchilarga ko'plab erkinlik berdi. Tikuvchilar o'lcham yoki tana shakli haqida qayg'urishlari shart emas edi. Kimonolar ayollar uchun ham, erkaklar uchun ham bir xil o'lchamda tikiladi. Ular har bir kishiga mos tushishi uchun buklanadi va qistiriladi. Qish uchun qalin matolar, yoz uchun esa yupqa zig'ir matolar ishlatilishi mumkin.

Kimonolar uzoq vaqt davomida juda mashhur bo'ldi. Ular ommalashib borgani sari, odamlar turli xil ranglar va matolarga qiziqa boshladilar. Ular yorqin matolarni fasllarga moslashtirdilar. Shuningdek, o'tmishda ranglarni ijtimoiy va siyosiy tabaqaga moslashtirish mumkin edi. Birovning kimonosiga qarab, uning jamiyatning qaysi qismiga mansubligini aytish mumkin edi.

G'arb dunyosida va boshqa mamlakatlarda odamlar ko'p aksessuarlar taqishadi. Ular uzuk va marjonlar kabi taqinchoqlar taqishlari mumkin. Ammo yaponlar bunday kiyinishmagan. Buning o'rniga, ular o'z uslubi va didini kimonolarining matosi bilan ifodalashgan. Kimono dizaynerlari matodan xuddi polotno (rism chizish matosi) kabi foydalanishgan. Rasmlar chizadigan rassom kabi, odamlar matolariga juda nafis tasvirlarni chizdirishgan. Ba'zi kimonolar hatto siyoh bilan bo'yalgan!

Jang maydonidagi jangchilar ham juda yorqin va rang-barang kimonolar kiyishgan. Asakrlar o'z kiyimlariga katta e'tibor berishgan. Ular dabdabali liboslar kiyishgan! Jangchining kimonosining rangi u armiyaning qaysi qismiga tegishli ekanligini ko'rsatgan. Bir vaqtlar kimonolar 25 xil mato qatlamidan iborat bo'lgan! 25 qavat kiyim kiyishni tasavvur qila olasizmi? Askar va hukumat amaldorlari o'z tabaqasini ko'rsatish uchun maxsus ranglarda kiyingan bo'lsa, oddiy fuqarolar o'z oilasini ko'rsatish uchun kimono kiyishgan. Ular kimonolariga o'z oilaviy tamg'alarini (gerb) tushirishgan. Turmushga chiqqan ayollarning kimono uslubi turmushga chiqmagan ayollarnikidan farq qilgan. Onalar qizlariga kimononi qanday kiyishni o'rgatishlari kerak bo'lgan. Hozirgi kunda kimononi qanday to'g'ri kiyishni o'rgatadigan maktablar mavjud.

Kimono Marosimi (Ceremony)

Kimonolar an'anaviy marosimlar uchun ham haligacha kiyiladi. Siz turmush qurayotgan yapon juftliklarini kimono kiygan holda ko'rishingiz mumkin. Ular dafn marosimlariga ham kiyiladi. Yaponlar dafn marosimlariga qora kimono kiyishadi. Va uylanayotgan erkaklar ham qora kimono kiyishadi. O'tmishda ayollar choy marosimlari kabi an'anaviy san'at turlari bilan shug'ullanayotganda kimono kiyishgan. Hozir ular ko'pincha yozgi festivallarga kiyiladi. G'arb dunyosi an'anaviy yapon kimonosini qabul qildi. Agar siz Qo'shma Shtatlardagi kiyim do'koniga kirib, kimono so'rasangiz, sizga uzun yengil matoli xalat berishadi.

Hozir kimonolar tikish uchun eng mashhur mato bu ipakdir. Ipak qimmat mato hisoblanadi. Matoning narxi bugungi kunda kimonolarni ancha kam ommabop qilib qo'ydi. Bir kimono sizga minglab dollarga tushishi mumkin! Bugun Yaponiyaga sayohat qilsangiz, o'tmishdagidek ko'p kimono ko'rmaysiz. Biroq, an'anaviy kimono mato dizaynerlari Yaponiyada juda muhim hisoblanadi. Ular noyob san'at asarlarini yaratadilar.
