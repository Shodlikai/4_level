The History Of Time

If you can read a clock, you can know the time of day. But no one knows what time itself is. We cannot see it. We cannot touch it. We cannot hear it. We know it only by the way we mark its passing. For all our success in measuring the smallest parts of time, time remains one of the great mysteries of the universe. One way to think about time is to imagine a world without time. There could be no movement, because time and movement cannot be separated. A world without time could exist only as long as there were no changes. For time and change are linked. We know that time has passed when something changes.

In the real world, the world with time, changes never stop. Some changes happen only once in a while, like an eclipse of the moon. Others happen repeatedly, like the rising and setting of the sun. Humans always have noted natural events that repeat themselves. When people began to count such events, they began to measure time. In early human history, the only changes that seemed to repeat themselves evenly were the movements of objects in the sky. The most easily seen result of these movements was the difference between light and darkness.

The sun rises in the eastern sky, producing light. It moves across the sky and sinks in the west, causing darkness. The appearance and disappearance of the sun was even and unfailing. The periods of light and darkness it created were the first accepted periods of time. We have named each period of light and darkness: one day.

People saw the sun rise higher in the sky during the summer than in winter. They counted the days that passed from the sun's highest position until it returned to that position. They counted three hundred and sixty-five days. We now know that is the time Earth takes to move once around the sun. We call this period of time a year.

Early humans also noted changes in the moon. As it moved across the night sky, they must have wondered: Why did it look different every night? Why did it disappear? Where did it go? Even before they learned the answers to these questions, they developed a way to use the changing faces of the moon to tell time. The moon was "full" when its face was bright and round and "new" when it was almost entirely dark. The early humans counted the number of times the sun appeared between full moons. They learned that this number always remained the same, about twenty-nine suns. Twenty-nine suns equalled one moon. We now know this period of time as one month.

Early humans hunted animals and gathered wild plants. They moved in groups or tribes from place to place in search of food. Then, people learned to plant seeds and grow crops. They learned to use animals to help them work, and for food. They found they no longer needed to move from one place to another to survive. As farmers, however, they had to plant crops in time to harvest them before winter. They had to know when the seasons would change. So, they were forced to develop calendars. No one knows when the first calendar was developed. But it seems possible that it was based on moons, or lunar months.

When people started farming, the wise men of the tribes became very important. They studied the sky. They gathered enough information so they could know when the seasons would change. They announced when it was time to plant crops.

The Domestication of Cats

For centuries, the common view of how domestication had occurred was that prehistoric people, realizing how useful it would be to have captive herds of food animals, began capturing wild animals and breeding them. Over time, by allowing only animals with "tame" characteristics to mate and produce offspring, human beings created animals that were less wild and more dependent upon people. Eventually this process led to the domestic farm animals and pets that we know today, many of which would fare quite badly in the wild, having lost their ancient survival skills and instincts.

Recent research suggests that this view of domestication is incomplete. Prehistoric human beings did capture and breed useful wild animals, and those species became tamer over time (they generally changed physically, too, developing larger bodies and smaller brains than their wild cousins). But specialists in animal behavior now think that domestication was not simply something people did to animals — the animals played an active part in the process. Wolves and wild horses, for example, may have taken the first steps in their own domestication by hanging around human settlements, feeding on people's garbage and crops and getting used to human presence and activity. Individual animals that were not too nervous or fearful to live near people produced offspring that also tolerated humans, making it easier for people to capture and tame them.

In this version, people succeeded in domesticating only animals that had already adapted easily to life around humans. Domestication required an animal that was willing to become domestic. The process was more like a dance with two partners than a triumph of humans over animals.

At first glance, the taming of cats seems to fit nicely into this new story of domestication. A traditional theory says that after prehistoric people in the Near East and Egypt invented agriculture and started farming, rats and mice gathered to feast on their stored grain. Wildcats, in turn, gathered at the same places to prey on the rats and mice. Over time, cats got used to people and people got used to cats, until at some point cats were tame. New studies of wildcats, however, seem to call this theory into question. Wildcats don't share hunting and feeding territories, and they don't live close to people or seek out human settlements as food sources. Experts do not know whether wildcats were partners in their own domestication. They do know that long after people had acquired domestic dogs, sheep, goats, cattle, and horses, they somehow acquired tame cats. By mating the least aggressive cats with one another, they produced animals with increasingly tame qualities.

Vaqt Tarixi

Agar soatni o'qiy olsangiz, kun vaqtini bilishingiz mumkin. Ammo hech kim vaqtning o'zi nima ekanligini bilmaydi. Biz uni ko'ra olmaymiz. Biz uni ushlay olmaymiz. Biz uni eshita olmaymiz. Biz uni faqat uning o'tishini belgilash usulimiz orqali bilamiz. Vaqtning eng kichik qismlarini o'lchashdagi barcha muvaffaqiyatlarimizga qaramay, vaqt koinotning buyuk sirlaridan biri bo'lib qolmoqda. Vaqt haqida o'ylashning bir usuli - vaqtsiz dunyoni tasavvur qilishdir. Harakat bo'lishi mumkin emas edi, chunki vaqt va harakatni ajratib bo'lmaydi. Vaqtsiz dunyo faqat o'zgarishlar bo'lmagan taqdirdagina mavjud bo'lishi mumkin edi. Chunki vaqt va o'zgarish bog'liqdir. Biror narsa o'zgarganda vaqt o'tganini bilamiz.

Haqiqiy dunyoda, vaqt mavjud bo'lgan dunyoda o'zgarishlar hech qachon to'xtamaydi. Ba'zi o'zgarishlar oy tutilishi kabi faqat vaqti-vaqti bilan sodir bo'ladi. Boshqalari esa quyoshning chiqishi va botishi kabi takroran sodir bo'ladi. Insonlar har doim o'zini takrorlaydigan tabiat hodisalarini qayd etib kelganlar. Odamlar bunday voqealarni sanashni boshlaganlarida, ular vaqtni o'lchashni boshladilar. Insoniyat tarixining boshlarida o'zini bir tekisda takrorlaydigan yagona o'zgarishlar osmondagi jismlarning harakatlari edi. Ushbu harakatlarning eng oson ko'rinadigan natijasi yorug'lik va qorong'ulik o'rtasidagi farq edi.

Quyosh sharqiy osmonda ko'tarilib, yorug'lik hosil qiladi. U osmon bo'ylab harakatlanadi va g'arbda botib, qorong'ulikni keltirib chiqaradi. Quyoshning paydo bo'lishi va g'oyib bo'lishi bir tekis va uzluksiz edi. U yaratgan yorug'lik va qorong'ulik davrlari birinchi qabul qilingan vaqt davrlari edi. Biz yorug'lik va qorong'ulikning har bir davrini nomladik: bir kun.

Odamlar yozda quyosh qishga qaraganda osmonda balandroq ko'tarilishini ko'rdilar. Ular quyoshning eng yuqori holatidan to u yana o'sha holatga qaytguniga qadar o'tgan kunlarni sanadilar. Ular uch yuz oltmish besh kunni sanadilar. Biz hozir bu Yerning quyosh atrofida bir marta aylanishi uchun ketadigan vaqt ekanligini bilamiz. Biz bu vaqt davrini yil deb ataymiz.

Qadimgi odamlar oydagi o'zgarishlarni ham qayd etishgan. U tungi osmon bo'ylab harakatlanar ekan, ular qiziqqan bo'lishlari kerak: Nega u har kecha boshqacha ko'rinar edi? Nega u g'oyib bo'ldi? U qayerga ketdi? Ular ushbu savollarning javoblarini o'rganishdan oldin ham, vaqtni aniqlash uchun oyning o'zgaruvchan yuzlaridan foydalanish usulini ishlab chiqdilar. Oy yuzi yorqin va dumaloq bo'lganda "to'lin" va deyarli butunlay qorong'i bo'lganda "yangi" edi. Qadimgi odamlar to'lin oylar orasida quyosh necha marta chiqqanini sanadilar. Ular bu raqam har doim bir xil, taxminan yigirma to'qqiz quyosh bo'lib qolishini bilib oldilar. Yigirma to'qqiz quyosh bir oyga teng edi. Biz hozir bu vaqt davrini bir oy deb bilamiz.

Qadimgi odamlar hayvonlarni ovlaganlar va yovvoyi o'simliklarni yig'ishgan. Ular oziq-ovqat izlab guruhlar yoki qabilalar bo'lib joydan joyga ko'chib yurishgan. Keyin odamlar urug' ekishni va ekin yetishtirishni o'rgandilar. Ular ishlashda yordam berish va oziq-ovqat uchun hayvonlardan foydalanishni o'rgandilar. Ular omon qolish uchun endi bir joydan ikkinchi joyga ko'chib yurishlari shart emasligini tushunib yetdilar. Dehqon sifatida esa, ular qishdan oldin hosilni yig'ib olish uchun ekinlarni o'z vaqtida ekishlari kerak edi. Ular fasllar qachon o'zgarishini bilishlari kerak edi. Shunday qilib, ular taqvim ishlab chiqishga majbur bo'ldilar. Birinchi taqvim qachon ishlab chiqilganini hech kim bilmaydi. Ammo bu oylar yoki qamariy oylarga asoslangan bo'lishi mumkin.

Odamlar dehqonchilikni boshlaganlarida, qabilalarning donishmandlari juda muhim bo'lib qoldilar. Ular osmonni o'rgandilar. Ular fasllar qachon o'zgarishini bilish uchun yetarli ma'lumot to'pladilar. Ular ekin ekish vaqti kelganini e'lon qildilar.

Mushuklarning Xonakilashtirilishi

Asrlar davomida xonakilashtirish qanday sodir bo'lganligi haqidagi umumiy qarash shundan iborat ediki, ibtidoiy odamlar oziq-ovqat hayvonlarining tutqun podalariga ega bo'lish qanchalik foydali bo'lishini anglab, yovvoyi hayvonlarni tutishni va ularni ko'paytirishni boshladilar. Vaqt o'tishi bilan, faqat "yuvosh" xususiyatlarga ega bo'lgan hayvonlarning juftlashishi va nasl berishiga ruxsat berish orqali, insonlar kamroq yovvoyi va odamlarga ko'proq bog'liq bo'lgan hayvonlarni yaratdilar. Oxir-oqibat, bu jarayon biz bugungi kunda biladigan xonaki qishloq xo'jaligi hayvonlari va uy hayvonlariga olib keldi, ularning ko'plari o'zlarining qadimiy omon qolish ko'nikmalari va instinktlarini yo'qotib, yovvoyi tabiatda juda yomon ahvolda qolgan bo'lar edilar.

So'nggi tadqiqotlar shuni ko'rsatadiki, xonakilashtirish haqidagi bu qarash to'liq emas. Ibtidoiy insonlar haqiqatan ham foydali yovvoyi hayvonlarni tutib ko'paytirishgan va bu turlar vaqt o'tishi bilan yuvoshroq bo'lib qolgan (ular odatda jismonan ham o'zgargan, yovvoyi qarindoshlariga qaraganda kattaroq tana va kichikroq miya rivojlangan). Ammo hayvonlar xulq-atvori bo'yicha mutaxassislar endi xonakilashtirish shunchaki odamlar hayvonlarga qilgan narsa emas deb o'ylashmoqda — hayvonlar jarayonda faol ishtirok etishgan. Masalan, bo'rilar va yovvoyi otlar odamlar yashaydigan joylarda aylanib yurib, odamlarning chiqindilari va ekinlari bilan oziqlanib, inson mavjudligi va faoliyatiga o'rganib qolib, o'zlarini xonakilashtirishdagi birinchi qadamlarni tashlagan bo'lishlari mumkin. Odamlarga yaqin yashashdan unchalik asabiy yoki qo'rqmaydigan alohida hayvonlar odamlarga toqat qiladigan nasllarni dunyoga keltirib, odamlar uchun ularni tutish va xonakilashtirishni osonlashtirgan.

Ushbu versiyada odamlar faqat odamlar atrofidagi hayotga oson moslashgan hayvonlarni xonakilashtirishga muvaffaq bo'lishgan. Xonakilashtirish xonaki bo'lishga tayyor hayvonni talab qilardi. Jarayon odamlarning hayvonlar ustidan g'alabasidan ko'ra ko'proq ikki sherikning raqsiga o'xshardi.

Bir qarashda, mushuklarning xonakilashtirilishi ushbu yangi xonakilashtirish hikoyasiga juda mos keladigandek tuyuladi. An'anaviy nazariyaga ko'ra, Yaqin Sharq va Misrdagi ibtidoiy odamlar dehqonchilikni ixtiro qilib, ekin yetishtirishni boshlaganlaridan so'ng, kalamushlar va sichqonlar ularning saqlangan donlari bilan ziyofat qilish uchun yig'ilishgan. Yovvoyi mushuklar esa o'z navbatida kalamush va sichqonlarni ovlash uchun xuddi shu joylarga to'planishgan. Vaqt o'tishi bilan mushuklar odamlarga, odamlar esa mushuklarga o'rganib qolgan, toki qaysidir nuqtada mushuklar xonaki bo'lib qolgan. Biroq, yovvoyi mushuklarning yangi tadqiqotlari ushbu nazariyani shubha ostiga qo'yadigandek. Yovvoyi mushuklar ov qilish va oziqlanish hududlarini bo'lishmaydi va ular odamlarga yaqin yashamaydi yoki inson yashash joylarini oziq-ovqat manbai sifatida qidirmaydi. Mutaxassislar yovvoyi mushuklar o'zlarini xonakilashtirishda sherik bo'lgan-bo'lmaganligini bilishmaydi. Ular shuni bilishadiki, odamlar xonaki itlar, qo'ylar, echkilar, qoramollar va otlarga ega bo'lganidan ancha vaqt o'tgach, ular qandaydir yo'l bilan xonaki mushuklarga ega bo'lishgan. Eng kam tajovuzkor mushuklarni bir-biri bilan juftlashtirish orqali ular tobora yuvosh xususiyatlarga ega hayvonlarni yetishtirdilar.
